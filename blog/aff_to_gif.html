How to Turn Affinity Artboards into a GIF

August 4 2024

Notes: Mac Specific, Terminal, Homebrew, Affinity Designer, File Conversion

If you want to turn your affinity artboards into a GIF, here is how I did it.

Open the Export Persona tab in Affinity. Your boards are called "slices" here. On the righthand panel, below your list of slices, near that little delete icon, is the Export Slices button. Export your slices into a new folder.

Now, open the terminal. I use Mac. Install imagemagick with homebrew.

brew install imagemagick

Once that's done, right-click on your folder, go down to Services then click Open Terminal at Folder.

A terminal will pop, up. Here's the command to make a gif, using all the images in the folder.

JPEG files in directory

magick -delay 160 -loop 0 $(find . -name "*.jpg" | sort) title.gif

PNG files in directory

magick -delay 160 -loop 0 $(find . -name "*.png" | sort) title.gif

Now there should be a new file title.gif in the folder with all your images.
